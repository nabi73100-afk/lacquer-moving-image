# Lacquer Moving Image Research

This repository develops an artistic and theoretical framework for understanding lacquer as a temporal and material language within moving image practices, including cinema, animation, and AI-generated visual systems.

Rather than treating lacquer solely as a traditional craft medium, this project explores its potential as a deep material structure shaping visual temporality, narrative memory, and cinematic perception.

In this context, lacquer becomes:

- A medium of temporal layering  
- A visual technology of historical continuity  
- A material ontology of cinematic time  
- A cross-media bridge between ancient craft and digital image systems  

---

## Research directions

- Lacquer aesthetics in cinematic temporality  
- Material time and visual narrative structures  
- AI-assisted moving image grounded in material philosophy  
- Animation as material ontology  
- Cross-media translation between lacquer practice and film language  
- Deep-time visual systems in contemporary media art  

---

## Long-term objectives

- Develop lacquer-based cinematic language  
- Establish theoretical foundations for material-driven moving image  
- Create experimental films and animation projects  
- Connect museum practice with cinematic production  
- Expand lacquer research into global media theory discourse
